    Paragraphs

    Paragraphs are opened with indents (not extra lines), thusly,

    How true were his words, and how wise! How fortunate it would have been for me if I had heeded them. But I was young, I was but seven years of age, and vain, foolish, and anxious to attract attention. I wrote the biography, and have never been in a respectable house since.

    General text is in paragraph tags. Paragraphs use a first-line indent.

    New paragraph continues with sample text, "They are the best-known unknown persons that have ever drawn breath upon the planet".

    Blockquotes

    Typography treats blockquotes as a standalone paragraph type. In this CSS, blockquotres are not a figure (if a quote is an illustration of text, then it can be created using QUOTE elements in a FIGURE)

    Within the blockquote, the paragraphs will be handled as usual i.e. indented.

    The two ways of contemplation are not unlike the two ways of action commonly spoken of by the ancients: the one plain and smooth in the beginning, and in the end impassable; the other rough and troublesome in the entrance, but after a while fair and even.

    So it is in contemplation: If a man will begin with certainties, he shall end in doubts; but if he will be content to begin with doubts he shall end in certainties.

    Adding the "blockparagraph" class alters the paragraphing style from indents to line separation,

    The two ways of contemplation are not unlike the two ways of action commonly spoken of by the ancients: the one plain and smooth in the beginning, and in the end impassable; the other rough and troublesome in the entrance, but after a while fair and even.

    So it is in contemplation: If a man will begin with certainties, he shall end in doubts; but if he will be content to begin with doubts he shall end in certainties.

    Blockquotes can have citations. The style is kept to a minimum. This is because citations can become complex, as in full citation of dates, titles, pages, and styles. This CSS will not enter into this field (niether does LaTeX),

    The two ways of contemplation are not unlike the two ways of action commonly spoken of by the ancients: the one plain and smooth in the beginning, and in the end impassable; the other rough and troublesome in the entrance, but after a while fair and even.

    Illustrations

    Illustrations are split into spanblacks (necessary), and figures (unnecessary). Both elements surround transformed non-phrasal items. Both HTML elements behave much the same.

    Illustrations can contain images, such as the display below, with bottom caption,

    and be framed. This figure has a top caption, which requires a class "ilsn-caption-top",

    ...or ruled,

    Book illustrations are nearly always default width, but 'wide', 'narrow', 'x-narrow'. and 'xx-narrow' figure sizes are available,

    Spanblocks

    Spanblocks are illustrations, like figures, but essential to text. They can contain any illustrative content such as images, objects, tables...

    Even quotes, in which case spanblocks are like blockquotes, but essential,

    Like figures, spanblocks can set narrow, the contents framed/ruled, cited, etc.

    Illustration contents

    Illustrations can contain several types of contents. The initial examples, above, used images. They can also contain diagrams (for HTML same as images), quotes, HTML objects etc. They can also include tables.

    Quotes

    A substantial quote can be viewed as a text illustration. This is created using a QUOTE elements in a FIGURE element,

    Tables

    Tables should be in an figure or spanblock. They currently have a light styling,

    Tables can have toprule/bottomrule. Classes go on the table element. Midrule also can go on TABLE element, but will only work if table has a header,

    Tables can be easy linespaced. Use 'addvspace' on a TD,

    And, as any figure/spanblock, tables can be captioned top,

    ...or bottom,

    Don't forget to close the 'article' or 'section'.

    WebBook offers a table of contents (TOC) layout. In the context of web organisation, a TOC cannot be the same as a book (page numbers are irrelevant). A TOC is assumed to be a (potentially long) list of links. But TOC style retains some structure and style from typography.

    The main TOC style is mostly a style-leveller that removes the usual list style of this CSS. Titles, font and level styling are assumed to be for further work,
